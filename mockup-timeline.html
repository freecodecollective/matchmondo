⚽ Match Time-Lapse — scrub the match 0′→90′

Jahan's idea: a slider under the formation pitch. An advanced feature, off by default — flip it on in Settings, then drag to watch subs come on/off, goals & cards land at the exact minute, and each goal replay the real shot (ball from → to, scorer's face at the spot they struck it).

Jahan "Could there be a slider on the bottom of the image showing the field, so we could scroll through minutes 0–90 to see when people subbed on or off, and when goals were scored… and if ESPN has data on how goals are scored — where the shot was from and to — you could animate those."
Try it: turn on Match Time-Lapse in the Settings phone (left) and the match view (right) switches from a static full-time lineup to the scrubber. Real Netherlands 5–1 Sweden data.

What animates
• Subs — the player going off shrinks & fades out of his slot; the player coming on pops in to the same spot. Scrub backward and it reverses.
• Red card — the sent-off player animates out and the team plays on a man down (his slot stays empty). This match had no reds, so it won't trigger here — the behavior is wired, not demoed.
• Goal — the ball travels along the real coordinates (where the shot was struck → where it crossed the line), and the scorer's face moves to the exact spot he shot from. Nothing else is moved — formations stay put.
• Speed — ½× / 1× / 2× / 4× on the play button; the clock holds for a beat on each goal so the replay can play.
Why players don't “run” — and what's real
An honest note, since it shaped the design.
ESPN's free feed gives the location of the ball / the action, never a position for all 22 players. So we deliberately don't fake player movement — the formation is static. The only position we move is real: on a goal the scorer's face goes to the coordinate the shot was actually taken from. Goals and shots carry a from → to; fouls, corners and offsides carry a spot; subs, cards and kickoffs carry no location. True all-player heatmaps/movement would need a tracking provider (Opta / StatsBomb / SkillCorner), which this feed isn't.
Data & performance — does this hammer ESPN?
No. A finished match's data is static, so it's fetched once and cached, then the whole replay runs locally — scrubbing makes zero network calls. It fits the pipeline you already have: a scheduled job hits ESPN, extracts a slim ~4 KB timeline (keyEvents + goal shot coords + rosters — not ESPN's 440 KB blob), and commits it to git as data/match-events/<eid>.json. The iOS app reads that static file like every other data/*.json. So ESPN gets hit by the job — roughly once per match after full-time (~104 matches all tournament), plus a few polls per live match if you want it to fill in during play. Users always read git, never ESPN.
