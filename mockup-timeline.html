⚽ Match Time-Lapse — scrub the match 0′→90′

Jahan's idea, built for real: drag the slider under the pitch to watch the lineup change through the match — subs come on and off, goals and cards pop the moment they happened, and each goal replays the shot exactly where it was taken.

Jahan "Could there be a slider on the bottom of the image showing the field, so we could scroll through minutes 0–90 to see when people subbed on or off, and when goals were scored… and if ESPN has data on how goals are scored — where the shot was from and to — you could animate those."
Yes on all of it. ESPN's match feed carries the exact minute of every goal, card and substitution, plus shot coordinates (where it was struck → where it crossed the line). This demo uses the real Netherlands 5–1 Sweden data. Press play, or grab the dot and scrub.

How it works
Everything here is driven off one ESPN match-summary fetch — no hand-entry. The same pieces already power the Formations and Lineups views, so wiring this into the iOS match screen is mostly UI.
What the feed gives us (per match):
• keyEvents — every goal, yellow/red, and substitution with its clock minute and the players involved (scorer + assist, on + off).
• commentary plays — for each goal, a shot start (x,y) and end (x,y) on a 0–100 pitch grid. That's the "from → to" animation.
• rosters — the starting XI with formation slots, so the time-lapse knows who began and where.

The scrubber logic: at minute T we start from the XI, apply every sub with minute ≤ T (the incoming player drops into the outgoing player's slot), and show each goal/card/assist marker once its minute passes. Crossing a goal forward replays the shot; tapping a goal tick jumps to and replays it.
