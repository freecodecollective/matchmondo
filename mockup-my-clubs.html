⚽ My Clubs — see how your boys are doing

"I'm an Arsenal fan. Where are my players, when do they play next, how are they doing?" A design exploration for surfacing your club's World Cup players inside MatchMondo.

Why this Most football fans support a club, not (or as well as) a country. During the World Cup the question "how's Saka doing?" is a club-fan question first. Right now you'd hunt: go to England → scroll the squad → find Saka. With a "My Clubs" feature, your Arsenal lads show up grouped together with their next fixture, latest stats, and a link to their detail page — one tap from home.
Where does it live?
Recommendation: a new "My Clubs" top-level sub-tab inside Stats, alongside Players / My Players / Groups. Familiar pattern, no new bottom-tab real estate, and the data shape (filterable player list) matches what Stats already does. Alternative: a card on Today's home that surfaces just your clubs' players playing today.
What does the user pick?
A small set of favorite clubs (Arsenal, Bayern, Boca…), not leagues. League-level (Premier League / La Liga) is too broad for the "how are my boys" question. We could still let users pick a league as a shortcut to favorite all of its top clubs, but the unit of storage is the club.
Where does the data come from?
Already in the app. The roster JSON in rosters.js has a club field for every WC26 player. No new fetches, no new backend. The lookup is "every roster player whose club is in my favorites." Players who change clubs mid-tournament are rare; we just trust the roster snapshot.
How many clubs can I add?
No hard cap. Most users will pick 1–3. The view still works at 10+ (just a longer scroll). We pin the most recently active club to the top by default; user can drag-reorder later.

Implementation cost: the data is already on the device. The only new pieces are (1) a club-name picker UI, (2) a `favoriteClubs` set in AppSettings (already in your local WIP), and (3) the "My Clubs" view itself — basically a grouped list off the rosters keyed by club. No backend changes. No new ESPN calls.

What this doesn't try to do: league standings, club-vs-club rivalries, or "which of your club's players are MOST IMPORTANT to their country." Those are interesting follow-ups but each adds real surface area; the MVP above is the answer to "how are my boys doing?" — nothing more.
