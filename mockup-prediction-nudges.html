Predictions on the Schedule — layout options

The same three sample matches are rendered four ways. Each variant shows: (1) a fixture where you've made a prediction, (2) one where you haven't, and (3) a played fixture. Pick the one that doesn't feel cluttered. Tapping any prediction element opens the Predictions tab; tapping a "Make a prediction" prompt opens Predictions for that specific match.

Settings toggle

Whichever variant we land on, the Settings page gets a switch to turn the schedule prediction surfaces off. Two related toggles, one parent + one nudge:

Predictions

Show predictions in schedule
Display your pick (or a prompt) on every match in the 3-day and Full Schedule views.
Show "Make a prediction" prompts
When off, only matches you've already predicted show your pick — no nudges on unpredicted matches.

My ranking from most to least recommended:

  1. F · Left rail — scannable column of "what you've picked," whole card taps, label is optional. Best balance of clarity + density.
  2. A · Inline row below the card — most legible and most obvious affordance; pays ~28px per match.
  3. G · Corner badge — lightest footprint that still surfaces the pick. A good "subtle" choice.
  4. E · Top banner — visually loud, can wear thin if 90% of cards have one.
  5. D · Bottom ribbon — most minimal. Picks blend into the card edge; weakest discoverability.
  6. I · Pick takes over — bold, but demotes kickoff time (which is core info) and risks confusion with the real scoreline.
  7. H · Per-team scores — clever mirror of a scoreline, but the "predict / predict" empty state reads oddly.
  8. C · In the score column — saves space but the tap target is too small.
  9. B · Right-side chip — competes with existing LIVE / group badges on narrow phones.
