### Spain 2-1 Belgium (QF, Los Angeles)

#### Result & Timeline
- Fabian Ruiz goal 30' -- Porro exchanged passes with teammates, fed the ball into the box, Courtois got a hand to the shot but couldn't hold it, Ruiz popped in and slapped it home *(Men in Blazers, Rog)*
- Charles De Ketelaere equalizer -- KDB pass unlocked the backline, Castagne delivered a cross, De Ketelaere headed home *(Men in Blazers, Rog; Men in Blazers, Rory Smith)*
- Mikel Merino winner 88' -- Kubasi took a mid-range shot, Lammens spilled it (ball slapped off his palms), Merino was there to blast it home *(Men in Blazers, Rog)*
- Merino had scored against Portugal in 6 minutes as a substitute; scored here in 2 minutes as a substitute *(Men in Blazers, Rog)*

#### Spain's Clean Sheet Streak
- Spain had not conceded a goal in the entire tournament entering this match -- five games *(Men in Blazers, Rog)*
- At kickoff, Spain's goalless streak stood at 609 minutes -- a tournament world record *(Men in Blazers, Rog)*
- Had not conceded in a tournament since Japan in Qatar 2022 *(Men in Blazers, Rog)*
- De Ketelaere's equalizer snapped the streak at 649 minutes *(Men in Blazers, Rog)*
- Unai Simon had made just two saves the entire tournament before this match *(Men in Blazers, Rog)*
- In the group stage, Spain conceded just 15 total shots with only three on target *(Men in Blazers, Rog)*
- Austria managed zero shots on target in the previous round *(Men in Blazers, Rog)*

#### Spain Unbeaten Run
- Spain were unbeaten in 35 games entering this match *(Men in Blazers, Rog)*

#### Belgium Injuries & Lineup
- Yuri Tielemans injured in the warm-up, unable to play *(Men in Blazers, Rory Smith)*
- Amadou Onana already out with an ACL injury suffered against the US; arrived at the stadium on crutches *(Men in Blazers, Rog)*
- Kevin De Bruyne started despite being 34 -- had been dropped for the US match, reinstated for the injured Onana *(Men in Blazers, Rog; Men in Blazers, Rory Smith)*
- Rory on KDB starting: "I was surprised to see him in just because he's 34. His knees don't really work anymore. Against Spain you're going to be chasing all the time." *(Men in Blazers, Rory Smith)*
- Belgium had not lost in 18 games entering this match *(Men in Blazers, Rog)*
- Jeremy Doku also started *(Men in Blazers, Rog)*

#### Courtois Injury
- Thibaut Courtois forced to leave the field after saving a shot (appeared to be from Morata/Fabel) with his thigh; looked routine initially but he was suddenly in agony, on his back, and limped off in tears *(Men in Blazers, Rog)*
- Courtois is 34 years old *(Men in Blazers, Rog)*
- Rory: "He might genuinely have another one in him. It's not impossible that he plays in 2030." *(Men in Blazers, Rory Smith)*
- Rory on Courtois's importance: "When Thibaut Courtois decides he's not conceding goals, he tends not to concede goals. It's really irritating but it's very effective." *(Men in Blazers, Rory Smith)*
- Injury appeared to be a dead leg *(Men in Blazers, Rory Smith)*
- Sen Lammens (Manchester United) came on as replacement *(Men in Blazers, Rog)*

#### Lammens & the Winning Goal
- Merino's winner: Lammens should have caught Kubasi's shot -- "It was a routine save not made" *(Men in Blazers, Rog)*
- Rory: "It was a regulation save. He should have caught it. It did bounce quite awkwardly... but for a professional that will be devastating." *(Men in Blazers, Rory Smith)*
- After the goal error, Lammens's next action was a simple clearance and he swiped at it -- "looked really panicky because it gets in their heads as soon as you've made a mistake" *(Men in Blazers, Rory Smith)*
- Camera cut immediately to Courtois on the bench who "slumped in agony as if the life had just left his soul" *(Men in Blazers, Rog)*

#### De Ketelaere's Tournament
- Three goals and one assist in the knockout rounds *(Men in Blazers, Rog)*
- Scored as many goals in this tournament as he did for Atalanta the entire club season *(Men in Blazers, Rory Smith)*
- Was not in the Belgium starting lineup at the start of the tournament; the US match "transformed him" and "established himself as Belgium's starting attacker" *(Men in Blazers, Rory Smith)*
- Was first a Flemish tennis champion at his country club at age 10 before switching to football *(Men in Blazers, Rog)*

#### KDB's Pass
- De Bruyne's pass for the equalizer: "It was muscle memory. Someone who's done it a million times before and sees the angles... you don't lose that aspect of it." *(Men in Blazers, Rory Smith)*
- Rory: "He can't do it all the time. He can't always be in position to do it all the time, but when he finds himself there, you don't lose it." *(Men in Blazers, Rory Smith)*
- This was likely KDB's last ever tournament game *(Men in Blazers, Rory Smith)*

#### Lamine Yamal
- Just one goal in the tournament *(Men in Blazers, Rog)*
- Was "one very, very frustrated figure in this game" *(Men in Blazers, Rog)*
- In the second half, was "holding on to the ball for way too long, trying things that were overly complicated" -- decided he had to solve the problem alone rather than playing collectively *(Men in Blazers, Rory Smith)*
- Got into a one-on-one trick battle with Doku in the first half -- "they both spent most of that game seeing who could do the best trick" -- Yamal "was really ineffective" *(Men in Blazers, Rory Smith)*
- Rory: "Spain's strength is collective. The idea is that you subsume yourself to the collective... Lamine's meant to be part of that group. He decided he didn't want to be. He wanted to be the hero and ended up doing very little." *(Men in Blazers, Rory Smith)*

#### Nico Williams
- Williams had been injured for a lot of the tournament, desperate to prove himself *(Men in Blazers, Rog)*
- Was beaten to the ball by centimeters by Merino on the winning goal *(Men in Blazers, Rog)*
- Rog: "Watch the goal again. You'll see Nico Williams' clear emotional reaction was just pure hearts shattering disappointment that he hadn't scored the goal." *(Men in Blazers, Rog)*
- Rory described it as "genuine annoyance that his teammate has scored. Very Cristiano Ronaldo." *(Men in Blazers, Rory Smith)*

#### Fabian Ruiz

#### Belgium's Approach

- Garcia said: "We're only 12 million people, but there's many, many millions around the world supporting our boys now after the Balogun incident." *(Men in Blazers, Rog)*

#### Spain's Style / Semi-Final Preview

### Argentina vs Switzerland Preview (QF, July 11)

#### Messi / MLS
- Messi is doing all this as an MLS player *(Men in Blazers, Rog)*
- Rog: "MLS often sonies players... their skills slowly evaporate" -- Messi is the exception *(Men in Blazers, Rog)*
- Rory: Messi's game "is not based on power that can go. It's not based on pace that can be robbed from you. It's based on his mind, his foot." *(Men in Blazers, Rory Smith)*
- Against Egypt, Messi tracked back once. "He did track back. I noticed it. I thought, wow, that's interesting. He can do it. You could see he did track back and then he realized he was doing it, got angry with himself and just stopped." *(Men in Blazers, Rory Smith)*

#### Argentina Form
- Back-to-back 3-2 scorelines including extra time win against Cape Verde and the comeback against Egypt (three goals in the final 15 minutes) *(Men in Blazers, Rog)*
- Argentina have not lost in 11 straight tournament matches stretching back to the 2-1 vs Saudi Arabia in 2022 *(Men in Blazers, Rog)*
- Scaloni post-Egypt: "Football is this, not just tactics and strategies. Those are important, but it's heart. That's what we have. Without heart, we would have been out." *(Men in Blazers, Rog)*

#### Switzerland Form & Style
- Switzerland have never beaten Argentina in 7 previous meetings: 5 losses, 2 draws *(Men in Blazers, Rog)*
- Beat Colombia on penalties in the previous round *(Men in Blazers, Rog)*
- Switzerland and Colombia combined for just 0.7 xG in their match *(Men in Blazers, Rog)*
- Switzerland's last shot on target vs Colombia was in the 32nd minute *(Men in Blazers, Rog)*
- Johan Manzambi out injured *(Men in Blazers, Rog)*
- Rory: "Switzerland are really good at knockout football... a core that's been together for probably six to eight years. They're quite versatile." *(Men in Blazers, Rory Smith)*
- Rory: "Switzerland are no emotion whatsoever. They are process mentality. They won't try to come out and attack. They will lie and wait and hope that Argentina make a mistake." *(Men in Blazers, Rory Smith)*

#### Match Official Rule
- English officials Anthony Taylor and Michael Oliver are in the referee pool but are barred from refereeing Argentina games because of the Falklands War (1982); Anthony Taylor was 3 years old at the time, Michael Oliver was not yet born *(Men in Blazers, Rory Smith)*
- A Portuguese official assigned to this match *(Men in Blazers, Rog)*

#### Potential England-Argentina Semi-Final
- If both win, they would meet in the semi-final in Atlanta *(Men in Blazers, Rory Smith)*
- Atlanta's stadium is completely open with no segregation between fan sections -- Rory: "That is not going to work with Argentina and England fans" *(Men in Blazers, Rory Smith)*
- At the Egypt match in Atlanta, Argentine fans were chanting "he who doesn't jump is English" *(Men in Blazers, Rory Smith)*
- England and Argentina never play each other -- no friendlies, they try not to encounter each other at all *(Men in Blazers, Rory Smith)*
