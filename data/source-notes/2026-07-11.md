## The Athletic: Haaland's English Roots (July 10/11, 2026)

### Birth and Early Childhood
- Erling Haaland was born in Leeds, West Yorkshire on July 21, 2000 *(The Athletic, Beren Cross and Mark Critchley)*
- Father Alfie Haaland had recently completed a transfer from Leeds United to Manchester City when Erling was born *(The Athletic, Beren Cross and Mark Critchley)*
- Haaland spent the first three to four years of his life in England *(The Athletic, Beren Cross and Mark Critchley)*
- Alfie Haaland retired in July 2003 through injury; family then returned to their homeland, living in the south-western Norwegian town of Bryne *(The Athletic, Beren Cross and Mark Critchley)*

### Bilingual Upbringing
- The Haaland family spoke English during the day and Norwegian in the evenings while living in England *(The Athletic, Beren Cross and Mark Critchley)*
- Youth coach Alf Ingve Berntsen (Bryne, coached Haaland ages 8-15): "When you are three or four years old and you are used to speaking English in the daytime, but Norwegian in the evenings, then you go back home to Norway, it's all about the Norwegian language. You are in kindergarten, and then in school. It would be different if he had come back to Norway when he was seven or eight. He would be more English." *(The Athletic, Beren Cross and Mark Critchley)*

### Why Norway Over England
- Haaland (to Goal.com, 2023): "I lived here (in the UK) for three and a half to four years, and I lived in Norway for such a long time, so then it was natural to choose Norway. You never know how it would be if maybe my father played longer in England or whatever. Maybe I would be English, I don't know, but I'm Norwegian and I'm proud of it." *(The Athletic, Beren Cross and Mark Critchley)*
- Berntsen: "His mum and dad are Norwegian, he left England when he was three or four, his grandparents and cousins, brother and sister, all of them are Norwegian. So it was theoretically possible (for him to play for England), but not in reality." *(The Athletic, Beren Cross and Mark Critchley)*

### England FA Never Seriously Pursued
- Gareth Southgate (2020, when Haaland was at Borussia Dortmund): "Obviously at the younger age groups, that wouldn't have been on my radar because, when he broke into first-team football, he was pretty much already in the Norwegian youth system. We recruit early, but we wouldn't have been into him when he was still in Yorkshire, that's for sure." *(The Athletic, Beren Cross and Mark Critchley)*
- Southgate: "He feels that allegiance to the country he's playing for now and you're always very respectful of that." *(The Athletic, Beren Cross and Mark Critchley)*
- A former England youth coach (anonymous, worked with the FA when Haaland was emerging as a teenager): "No. We were never getting him. That wasn't going to happen. They were who his dad had played for, and that was what his ambition was." *(The Athletic, Beren Cross and Mark Critchley)*
- Same coach: "There was no point in even trying. It was sort of tongue-in-cheek, but never on a serious level, because we come across a lot of players at those ages who you could potentially get." *(The Athletic, Beren Cross and Mark Critchley)*
- Another FA source (talent identification): Haaland did not seriously come onto their radar until he was around 17 *(The Athletic, Beren Cross and Mark Critchley)*

### Haaland's Affection for English Clubs
- Berntsen: "He was proud of all his (father's) former clubs: Nottingham Forest, Leeds and (Manchester) City. All three have been part of Erling's history, and Leeds is special since he was born there." *(The Athletic, Beren Cross and Mark Critchley)*
- Michael Vigrestad (Haaland's junior-level teammate and friend in Bryne, writing for The Athletic last month): Haaland would wear Man City or Leeds shirts when they played together at weekends *(The Athletic, Beren Cross and Mark Critchley)*
- Haaland (2017, aged 16, quoted in Aftenposten): "I want to be the best (footballer) possible. The dream is to win the Premier League with Leeds. In addition, the goal is to become a better player than my dad was. I hope to get more caps than he did." *(The Athletic, Beren Cross and Mark Critchley)*

### Stuart Dallas Anecdote
- Former Leeds player Stuart Dallas: Haaland whispered "Marching on together" (Leeds United's long-time fan anthem) in his ear as the pair swapped shirts after an international between Norway and Northern Ireland *(The Athletic, Beren Cross and Mark Critchley)*

### Galatasaray Salute
- In January 2026, Haaland scored against Galatasaray for Man City in the Champions League; as he celebrated, he momentarily put a clenched fist across his chest in front of the opposition supporters -- the salute Leeds fans hold dear worldwide *(The Athletic, Beren Cross and Mark Critchley)*
- Context: In 2000, two Leeds supporters were murdered in Istanbul on the eve of a UEFA Cup semi-final first-leg against Galatasaray *(The Athletic, Beren Cross and Mark Critchley)*

### Leeds Tried to Sign Haaland (2018)
- In 2018, when Haaland was with Norwegian side Molde, Leeds tried to bring him back to his hometown club *(The Athletic, Beren Cross and Mark Critchley)*
- He was given a tour of Elland Road stadium and the training ground *(The Athletic, Beren Cross and Mark Critchley)*
- Red Bull Salzburg ultimately blew Leeds out of the water with their offer *(The Athletic, Beren Cross and Mark Critchley)*

### Alfie Haaland Career Detail
- Alfie Haaland played for Nottingham Forest, Leeds United, and Manchester City *(The Athletic, Beren Cross and Mark Critchley)*
