# Source Notes -- July 9, 2026

## France 2-0 Morocco (QF, Gillette Stadium, Boston)

### Result & Match Flow
- France 2, Morocco 0 -- quarterfinal at Gillette Stadium, Boston *(Men in Blazers, Rog)*
- 89 degrees at kickoff *(Men in Blazers, Rog)*
- First half: France took 13 shots to Morocco's 1; France had 1.87 xG at halftime *(Men in Blazers, Rog)*
- Morocco's only first-half shot came in injury time -- Hakimi free kick about 10 feet wide *(Men in Blazers, Rog)*
- Match momentum graph described as "so one-sided it looked like someone missing an entire row of teeth" *(Men in Blazers, Rog)*
- Second half became a stalemate in the heat; Morocco tried to nick a goal but lacked a potent striker without injured Ismael Sabari *(Men in Blazers, Rog)*
- Sabari had 3 goals in the tournament before injury *(Men in Blazers, Rog)*

### Mbappe Penalty (saved) & Goal (60')
- Penalty awarded after Hakimi misplayed the ball, Desire Doue pounced, nicked the ball to Mbappe in the box, and makeshift center defender Mazraoui hacked him down *(Men in Blazers, Rog)*
- Referee pointed to the penalty spot immediately -- "I don't think I've seen a referee point to the penalty spot quicker" *(Men in Blazers, Rory Smith)*
- Argentinian refereeing crew -- much pre-match discussion about whether France would accept it, but the penalty was so clearcut it was irrelevant *(Men in Blazers, Rog)*
- Referee then delayed the penalty for 3 minutes 10 seconds in the heat -- pointing at the ball on its spot, checking studs, general time-wasting *(Men in Blazers, Rog)*
- Mbappe's penalty was poor -- "less a penalty, more like a little tinkle" -- Bono saved it comfortably, picked it up "as if it was a slow roller that was passed to him" *(Men in Blazers, Rory Smith)*
- Mbappe scored the opening goal on 60 minutes -- picked up the ball on the left side of the box, took a touch, used a body shield, and unleashed "an absolute poem of an arcing curve" around Bono into the net *(Men in Blazers, Rog)*
- 8th goal of this tournament for Mbappe *(Men in Blazers, Rog)*
- 20 goals in 20 tournament games all-time for Mbappe *(Men in Blazers, Rog)*
- Now just one goal behind Messi's all-time tournament record, and Mbappe is 12 years younger *(Men in Blazers, Rory Smith)*

### Dembele Goal (2-0)
- Dembele given time and space to march forward -- "very un-Morocco" -- no challenges, rolled the ball into the corner of the net *(Men in Blazers, Rory Smith)*
- Described as a "worm burner" *(Men in Blazers, Rog)*
- 5th goal of the tournament for Dembele *(Men in Blazers, Rog)*
- In previous tournaments, 5 goals would be enough to win the Golden Boot; in this one, Dembele is not even in the top five *(Men in Blazers, Rog)*
- Dembele scored a hat-trick earlier in the tournament (vs Norway) *(Men in Blazers, Rory Smith)*
- Bono got his hand to Dembele's shot but the disguise on the shot beat him *(Men in Blazers, Rog)*

### Bono Penalty Record
- Yassin Bono: in the last 10 penalty shootouts, has saved 12 of his 42 penalties *(Men in Blazers, Rog)*
- At the tournament level: only been beaten by 2 out of 9 penalties faced; saved 3, 4 missed the target *(Men in Blazers, Rog)*
- Moved to Al-Hilal in the Saudi league after the 2022 tournament; described as "almost going into cold storage, saving himself for marriage aka tournaments" *(Men in Blazers, Rog)*
- Previously played for Sevilla; was a "slow burner" who reached top level in his late 20s/early 30s, by which point big clubs looked younger *(Men in Blazers, Rory Smith)*

### Stutter-Step Penalties
- This tournament has been a showcase of disastrous stutter-step penalty technique *(Men in Blazers, Rog)*
- Mbappe's miss compared to Messi's recent penalty miss -- "if missing penalties is good enough for Messi, steal from the greats" *(Men in Blazers, Rory Smith)*

### Morocco Identity & Investment
- 19 of Morocco's 26 players were born outside Morocco -- in France, the Netherlands, Spain, Canada, Belgium *(Men in Blazers, Rog)*
- 23% of all players at this tournament were born outside the nation they represent *(Men in Blazers, Rog)*
- 5 million Moroccans living abroad; squad composition: 7 from home, 6 Spain, 6 France, etc. *(Men in Blazers, Rog)*
- Morocco's rise is a deliberate national project: investment from King Mohammed VI, state-of-the-art training facility, national academy, regional training centers, stadium redevelopment, construction of 1,000 amateur fields *(Men in Blazers, Rog)*
- Morocco described as "Africa's first superpower" in football -- first genuine African heavyweight contender to potentially win the tournament *(Men in Blazers, Rory Smith)*
- Morocco's academy system modeled on Clairefontaine, the French system *(Men in Blazers, Rory Smith)*
- Ayyoub Bouaddi played for Morocco but captained the French U-21 side to a 2-1 win over Iceland just 101 days earlier *(Men in Blazers, Rog)*
