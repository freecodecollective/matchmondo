## Match 76: Brazil 2-1 Japan (FT)
**Venue:** NRG Stadium, Houston, TX | **Scorers:** Sano 42' (JPN), Casemiro 64' (BRA), Martinelli 90+6' (BRA)

### Key Facts
- Martinelli's 96th-minute winner was the latest winner scored in regulation time of a WC knockout game since 1966 (per Opta)
- Ancelotti is the first foreigner to coach Brazil at a World Cup
- Casemiro booked early for foul outside box; may have contributed to his reluctance to track Sano's run for the goal
- Casemiro scored more headers than any PL player last season (8; Van Dijk next with 5)
- Lucas Paqueta injured just before halftime; replaced by Endrick — effectively moved to 4-2-4
- Vinicius Jr had 4 goals in first 3 games; was frustrated by Japan's 5-4-1 in first half
- Zion Suzuki (GK, born in New Jersey to Ghanaian father and Japanese mother) made save of the match tipping Vinicius shot onto post
- Kaishu Sano ranked 3rd in Bundesliga for distance covered last season (401.1 km)
- Martinelli deployed as inside forward / left-sided No.8 (not his usual Arsenal winger role) — Ancelotti's choice
- Brazil's youngest starting defender was Gabriel (28); Danilo 34, Marquinhos 32, Douglas Santos 32
- Casemiro injury concern — forced off late, status TBD for R16

### Quotes
- Ancelotti (on no outstanding favorite): "There's no outstanding favourite" for the tournament
- Casemiro: "We had to be patient with the ball, move them around... It was a mental test."
- Bruno Guimaraes: "Ancelotti asked us to put players in the box and try to cross. Everything was perfect in the second half."
- Martinelli: "At Arsenal, I don't play in that position, but I can do it. The coach has talked to me about it."
- Gabriel Magalhaes: "The coach always tries to transmit calmness to us."

### Additional Details (BBC, Sam Drury)
- Sano arguably lucky to still be on pitch — clumsy challenge while already on yellow card before scoring
- Brazil put in 28 crosses in the second half (less than 2 minutes between each on average)
- Tomiyasu goalline block to deny Casemiro soon after interval
- Ao Tanaka lost ball on corner of own box → Guimaraes → Martinelli for the winner
- Japan's wait for a knockout win at a World Cup goes on
- BBC Player of the Match: Sano (7.83 avg rating); Martinelli 6.78, Vinicius 6.25
- Attendance: 68,777

### Tactical
- First half: Japan's relentless press overran Brazil's midfield; Sano intercepted loose Danilo pass, burst past Casemiro
- Halftime: Ancelotti brought on Endrick for injured Paqueta, went 4-2-4; Vinicius and Rayan hugged touchlines to stretch Japan
- Second half transformation: more crossing opportunities, Japan's press neutralized by width
- Ancelotti's composure: after 96th-min winner, he paused and turned to speak to assistant Paul Clement while everyone else celebrated; sent sub on to lock things down

### Next

## Match 74: Germany 1-1 Paraguay (PEN 3-4, Paraguay advance)
**Venue:** Gillette Stadium, Foxborough (Boston), MA | **Scorers:** Enciso 42' (PAR), Havertz 54' (GER) | **Attendance:** 84F/29C heat

### Key Facts
- Germany lost their first ever World Cup penalty shootout (had won previous 4; scored 15 consecutive WC PKs before Havertz miss)
- 4th biggest knockout shock by rankings since 1992 (Germany 12th, Paraguay 33rd)
- Germany have not won a WC knockout game since winning the 2014 final
- Germany have now failed at 3 consecutive World Cups (2018 group exit, 2022 group exit, 2026 R32 exit)
- Germany's win rate at WCs: 75% through 2014, only 40% since
- 10 consecutive WC games in which Germany have conceded
- Jonathan Tah's header disallowed by VAR — Waldemar Anton briefly impeded GK Orlando Gill; controversial call (referee from Morocco, VAR from Nicaragua)
- Paraguay emerged late for second half, leaving Germany waiting on pitch
- Miguel Almiron available after serving 1-match ban for being first player red-carded under new mouth-covering rule (vs Turkey)
- Julio Enciso nearly missed squad entirely — carted off with hands over face in final warmup friendly vs Nicaragua; hamstring fears proved misplaced
- Enciso scored 12 times for Strasbourg in Ligue 1 last season
- First ever goal for Paraguay in WC knockout stage
- Nagelsmann started Deniz Undav for Musiala (boldest selection of tournament); moved to ~4-4-2; didn't work

### PK Details
- GER: Havertz SAVED (Gill), Kimmich scored, Musiala scored, Woltemade SAVED (Gill), Tah BLAZED OVER
- PAR: Mauricio scored, Gomez scored, Galarza scored, Sanabria WIDE, Balbuena SAVED (Neuer), Canale scored (winner)
- Orlando Gill saved 2 for Paraguay; Neuer saved 1 for Germany

### Quotes
- Nagelsmann: "This is the third elimination in a row and that means we are not part of the first-class teams anymore."
- Nagelsmann: "I won't step down. If the DFB want me to continue, I'll continue."
- Havertz: "I want to apologise because this is my second World Cup and we have failed twice."
- Amiri: "We have a lot of young players with a lot of quality. But at the moment, to think about the future is the wrong decision."

### Tactical
- Nagelsmann switched from usual setup: dropped Musiala for Undav, went 4-4-2 seeking more chance creation + protection on turnovers
- Didn't work — defending still littered with individual errors
- Brought on Goretzka for Nmecha at HT — added physical force to ponderous midfield
- Reverted to pre-tournament XI by second half (Musiala on for Undav)
- Paraguay defended superbly: centre-backs Canale and Gomez headed away everything despite being half a foot shorter than German subs
- Germany lacked fit left-backs after Schlotterbeck injury

### Penalty Shootout Psychology (Geir Jordet, The Athletic)
- ~20% of WC knockout games end in shootouts; expect 6-7 this tournament
- Gold standard: pre-set ranking of takers communicated directly; Scaloni in 2022 spent only 20 seconds nominating 5 players
- In 2022, all 5 shootouts won by team whose head coach was primary communicator in huddle
- Teams who spent shortest time on logistics/plans won their shootout (small sample)
- England pioneered "buddy system" in 2024: each player assigned as taker or buddy escort
- Goalkeeper-dependent (stutter) kicks vs goalkeeper-independent kicks; countermeasure: GK fake movements
- Morocco's Bounou masters double-fakes; has thwarted Toney, Haaland, Jimenez
- France's Maignan successfully faked vs Strand Larsen in this WC (France-Norway)
