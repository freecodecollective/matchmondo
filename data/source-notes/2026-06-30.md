- Haaland scored the winner in the 86th minute
- Norway's first-ever victory in the knockout stage
- Haaland now has 60 international goals in 53 appearances (age 25)

## Mexico 2-0 Ecuador (R32, Azteca)

### Key Facts
- First time since 1986 that Mexico have won a knockout game (only their 2nd ever knockout win)
- Mexico have won all 4 games on home turf without conceding a goal
- Mexico have not conceded a first-half goal in 15 consecutive tournament matches, stretching back to 2014. Last first-half goal conceded: Tevez (26') and Higuain (33') for Argentina in the 2010 R16. That's 16 years.
- With Canada also through, USA can make it a clean sweep for host nations
- Azteca staged finals in 1970 and 1986
- All quarter-finals onwards are in the U.S. (FIFA decision, revenue potential)
- Scorers: Julian Quinones (ran from own half, collected long pass, smashed into top corner), Raul Jimenez (rifled top bins from just outside the box, 31')
- Gilberto Mora (17 years 259 days) became youngest player to start a knockout game since Pele (17y 239d) in 1958
- Mora was "clean and decisive on the ball" before being subbed at 59'
- Ecuador complained to tournament organizers before the game after videos emerged of Mexico fans staging an impromptu fan fest outside their team hotel the previous night
- Kickoff delayed one hour due to thunderstorms/lightning in Mexico City
- Hydration break controversy: organizers said no cooling breaks due to the delay, but the referee stopped play around 23' for one anyway; the "no hydration breaks" line was later removed from the official statement
- Piero Hincapie (Arsenal, Ecuador) sent off in stoppage time for covering his mouth while confronting Santi Gimenez — second player sent off for this offence after Almiron vs Turkey
- Covering-mouth red card rule was added after a Vinicius Jr / Prestianni incident in Champions League (Feb 2026); IFAB extraordinary meeting made it a sending-off offence
- Obed Vargas: "If you ask me, we should be making the final"
- Rene Tovar (Record editor): "People are trying to inflate a product that's only average. We need to keep our feet on the ground."
- Mexico will face England or DR Congo in R16 at Azteca on July 5
- Nearly 40 million Mexicans living in the U.S.; national team is the link that keeps them close to home

### Tactical
- The noise and energy inside the stadium overwhelmed Ecuador in the early stages
- Mexico confident their supporters in U.S. could turn a potential QF in Miami into a home game
- Erik Lira (returning from injury) perhaps Mexico's most important player — dominated midfield
- Ranfle Rangel excellent in goal; Vasquez and Montes brick-wall solid at CB
- Quinones and Alvarado provided vastly different but equivalent threats wide
- Javier Aguirre has created a "family atmosphere"
- Mexico lack pace (Quinones aside) and don't have unimpeachable star power, but scrappiness and collective effort may be their biggest strength
