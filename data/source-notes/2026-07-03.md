# Source Notes — 2026-07-03 (R32 Day 7)

## Match 86: Argentina 3-2 Cabo Verde (AET)
**Venue:** AT&T Stadium, Arlington, TX | **Scorers:** Messi 28' (ARG), Duarte 59' (CPV), L. Martinez 91' (ARG), Cabral 103' (CPV), L. Martinez (ARG)

### Key Facts
- Largest gap between two teams in knockout round history: #1 Argentina vs #59 Cabo Verde
- Argentina were 92% favorites pre-match
- Messi's 30th Men's World Cup match — a record
- First player since the 1960s to score in five straight knockout games
- Messi's 20th World Cup goal
- Messi's 9th World Cup assist (all-time), 2nd behind Pelé
- Argentina had allowed only 2 goals in last 10 games before this — Cabo Verde scored 2
- Cabo Verde had lost just 1 of their last 18 games entering the match

### Messi's Opening Goal (28')
- Lisandro Martinez arced ball in; Messi ran off his defender
- First touch at speed outside of his boot — "the touch of a tender lover" *(Men in Blazers, Rog)*
- Second touch: rasping left-footed smash finish past the keeper

### Cabo Verde Equalizer (59') — Double Nutmeg
- Mendez charged down the right, nutmegged a defender, fed Duarte
- Duarte cut back, lashed shot through legs of Lisandro Martinez (second nutmeg), past Dibu Martinez
- "One of the most astonishing moments I've seen in knockout World Cup football" *(Men in Blazers, Rog)*
- Compared to UMBC upsetting #1 Virginia in 2018 March Madness

### Vzinia's Heroics
- 8 saves on the day, including stopping Messi one-on-one (with his groin)
- Made more dribbles than Cristiano Ronaldo had in the entire tournament
- Another stunning free-kick save from Messi
- Still without a club; MLS DP signing speculated

### Extra Time
- Lisandro Martinez scored (91') — corner flicked on, ball dropped unmarked at far post
- Sydney Lopez Cabral equalized AGAIN (103') — cut inside from distance, curled past Dibu Martinez from ~25 yards
- Cabral's last transfer was for $950,000 (plays in Spanish second division); ran to find his partner in the stands
- Argentina scored the winner to make it 3-2
- Scaloni (Argentina coach) covered his face with hands after winner — stress he'd been masking poured out

### Cape Verde Legacy
- Betty Clever: "When you think about the 2026 World Cup, you're 100% going to be thinking about Cape Verde"
- Rory Smith: "This will be Vzinia's World Cup and Cape Verde's World Cup" — compared to Cameroon/Roger Milla in 1990
- Peter Lopez: found on LinkedIn; first Irish League player to play in a World Cup; doesn't speak Portuguese
- Pre-match quote from Sydney Lopez Cabral: "If we start to think 'oh it's Messi,' then we're gonna lose our minds" — afterward all Cape Verde players got photos with Messi
- Rory on what Cape Verde proved: the gap between elite club footballers and second-division players "is not that big — it's often not to do with talent, it's to do with luck, injury, commitment"

### Argentine Fan Culture
- Rog on Argentine celebrations vs English: "It feels like an evangelical church — tears, prayers, religious collective ecstasy" vs English celebrations which are "a release of anger, bellowing, Hulk transformation poses"
- Rory: "the genesis of the Wayne's World 'we're not worthy' thing — they kind of mean it. Messi is an almost sanctified figure"
- Argentine fans "looked like they had seen a ghost" when Cabo Verde equalized — echoes of pre-2022 Argentina that "always managed to find a way to mess everything up"
- AJ Piano (chat): "The atmosphere after the game was somehow evocative of both teams having won"

## Match 88: Egypt 1-1 Australia (Egypt win 4-2 on penalties)
**Venue:** AT&T Stadium, Arlington, TX | **Scorers:** Ashure (EGY), Hani OG (AUS)

### Key Facts
- Egypt's first ever World Cup knockout win; hadn't made it past group stage since 1934
- Mo Salah back in squad — free agent, returning from hamstring; "not fully fit" *(Rory Smith)*
- Rory attended this game: "There were three teams playing — Egypt, Australia, and Liverpool" — most fans came to see Salah in Liverpool shirts, including knock-off Liverpool shirts in Arabic
- Own goal: Muhammad Hani headed into own net — his second own goal of the tournament; 13th own goal of the tournament overall (tournament record)
- Australia hadn't scored in 250 minutes before this game

### Penalty Shootout
- Australia swapped GK: took out Patrick Beach, put in Matty Ryan (echoing Van Gaal bringing in Tim Krul in 2014 vs Costa Rica); Beach "has stopped more expected goals than any other keeper in the tournament so far" *(Men in Blazers, Betty Clever)*
- Egyptian players gathered around a laptop to study Ryan's penalty movement — "amazing photograph"
- Ryan didn't save a single penalty; Rory's theory: "goalkeepers who are sent on to save penalties don't save penalties — Tim Krul did it once and loads of people decided it's a thing, but it's a trick"
- Harry Souttar missed his penalty ("birded" it — MiB's running term for a skied penalty)
- Lucas Harrington (18, Colorado Rapids, subject of Barcelona interest) missed decisive final penalty — hit bar
- Mo Salah's penalty: ran up, slowed down for a "penanka" (stutter step), sat Matty Ryan down; turned around and grinned at Ryan — "so nice to see Mo Salah smile wholly and warmly with his entire body" *(Rory Smith)*

### Mo Salah's Emotions
- Celebrated wearing a pharaoh hat (a "nemes")
- Tears: mix of relief (first knockout win), pride (captaining the team that broke through), and sadness (one year since Diego Jota's death)
- Rory: "He's been incredibly open... really felt his feelings, allowed himself to live his feelings in public — there's a really crucial lesson for everybody in that"
- Lewis Diaz (Colombia, later that day) did Jota's signature celebration after scoring — Jota's memory "hangs heavy over so many facets of this World Cup"

### Australia
- One of youngest squads (average age ~25); "Australian football is in a really good place" *(Betty Clever)*
- Betty: "They had one of the youngest squads; compare that to Egypt who had one of the oldest — they're only going to get better"

## Match 87: Colombia 1-0 Ghana
**Venue:** Arrowhead Stadium, Kansas City | **Scorer:** Aras (COL) | **Temp:** ~89°F at kickoff

### Key Facts
- Ghana manager Colus Kiraz: his 6th World Cup managing; previously managed Colombia for 22 months (2019+); walked up in gold chain, sweater draped over shoulders — "looked like a New York golf who dabbles in waste management" *(Rog)*
- Both teams had just 15 shots total in group stages — lowest of any team at the tournament
- Jean Cordoba (Colombia) injured in 5th minute pulling an opponent's shirt, tore his groin; on came Luis Suarez (Sporting Lisbon striker, 38 goals last season)
- First time in World Cup history both teams made a substitution in first 15 minutes
- Goal: Suarez cross, Lewis Diaz streaked across, Jean Aras one-timed it home
- James Rodriguez hooked at halftime
- Diaz had ball in net but offside — did Jota's signature celebration in tribute
- Ghana GK Lawrence Ati-Zigi made 8 saves, kept Ghana in the game
- Antoine Semenyo: 21 goals on the club season, zero shots on target for the entire World Cup — "shackled with defensive duties in the Kiraz system" *(Rog)*
- Colombia face Switzerland in R16
