## Balogun Red Card Analysis (The Athletic)

### The Incident
- 64th minute: Balogun and Muharemovic competed for a high ball; Balogun's trailing left foot came down on Muharemovic's ankle
- Initial yellow card upgraded to red after VAR review — "serious foul play"
- Balogun walked off slowly, visibly stunned; teammates consoled him in the tunnel

### Expert Analysis
- Graham Scott (former Premier League referee): "Unlucky but inevitable" — called it a "natural movement" that looks far worse in slow motion
- Scott: VAR slow-motion replay distorts actions — makes incidental contact look deliberate
- Scott: "If the referee sees it in real time, it might stay as a yellow. But once VAR gets involved and you see it frame by frame..."
- Multiple analysts agreed the contact was unintentional but met the threshold for serious foul play under the laws

### Suspension Impact
- Red cards at the tournament cannot be appealed — FIFA Article 66.4 prohibits appeals for on-field red cards
- Balogun automatically suspended for the Round of 16 vs Belgium (July 6, Seattle, 5:00 PM PT)
- US will need to find a replacement striker — Pepi or Sargent likely options
- Balogun could return for a potential quarterfinal

*Source: The Athletic (Adam Crafton, Tom Bogert, Ben Sheldon; Graham Scott — referee analysis)*
