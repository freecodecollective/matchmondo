# Source Notes — 2026-07-01 (R32 Day 5)

## Match 82: Belgium 3-2 Senegal (AET)
**Venue:** Seattle Stadium | **Scorers:** Diarra 28' (SEN), Sarr 52' (SEN), Lukaku 87' (BEL), Tielemans 89' (BEL), Tielemans 125' pen (BEL)

### Key Facts
- Biggest comeback of the 2026 tournament: Belgium rallied from 2-0 down
- Tielemans' 125th-minute penalty is the latest goal in tournament finals history
- Lukaku and Tielemans scored within 159 seconds (87' and 89') to level from 2-0 down
- De Bruyne and Doku both substituted after just 55 minutes — seemingly out of desperation by Garcia
- Penalty awarded after VAR review: Lamine Camara's sliding challenge caught Tielemans' left boot at the near post
- Senegal's Pathe Ciss went down injured on the penalty spot after a tap from Diego Moreira, delaying the penalty by over 7 minutes
- Lukaku held the ball as decoy before handing to Tielemans to take the penalty
- Tielemans whipped his penalty high into the top right corner
- First time this tournament a team recovered from 2-0 down
- Recalls Belgium's 2018 R16 comeback from 2-0 down vs Japan

### Senegal
- Sarr hit the left post twice before Diarra's opener
- Sarr's goal: controlled Moussa Niakhate's long pass with his chest over left shoulder, waited for drop, thundered into net
- Sarr scored his 4th goal in 4 matches — two shy of Mbappe (tournament-leading 6)
- Sarr scored 21 goals for Crystal Palace last season
- Senegal's rotational front three: Sarr, Mane, Ndiaye
- Midfield trio: Diarra, Pape Gueye, Idrissa Gueye
- Senegal still awaiting CAS ruling on overturned AFCON final — African Football Federation Appeal Board ruled 3-0 forfeit win for Morocco; Senegal walked off pitch in that final after disputed VAR penalty

### Belgium
- Rudi Garcia is Belgium manager
- Lukaku came on at half-time
- Trossard's cross for the equaliser: GK Mory Diaw leapt off his line and got nowhere near it, headed in by Tielemans
- Belgium meet the winner of USA vs Bosnia in the R16
- USMNT lost to Belgium 5-2 in March friendly
- Belgium has looked old and stagnant for significant chunks of all four games

### Club World Cup Trivia (from Athletic)
- Real Madrid players have scored 13 tournament goals (most by any club)
- PSG players: 11 goals
- Joint 3rd: Sunderland and Crystal Palace — 8 goals each
- Sunderland scorers: Brobbey (Netherlands, 3), Angulo (Ecuador, 1), Isidor (Haiti, 1), Xhaka (Switzerland, 1), Diarra (Senegal, 1) — five different Sunderland players have scored
- Crystal Palace scorers: Sarr (Senegal, 4), Munoz (Colombia, 2), Kamada (Japan, 2)
- Five Sunderland scorers is one behind Tottenham (2018) and Man Utd (2014) for most different scorers from one English club at a tournament

- USA hasn't beaten a top European team in more than a decade
- USA has a yearslong losing streak vs European teams
- Belgium's Trossard, De Bruyne, Doku are "a clear step above anybody in the U.S. squad"

## Match 80: England 2-1 DR Congo
**Venue:** Atlanta | **Scorers:** Cipenga (CON), Kane 75' (ENG), Kane 86' (ENG)

### Key Facts — Kane heroics
- Kane's 83rd and 84th goals for England (next highest: Rooney with 53)
- Kane's 19th and 20th major tournament goals — another England record, far ahead of the field
- Kane now has 13 tournament goals, joint 6th all-time — six behind Mbappe, seven behind Messi
- Kane's 2nd goal clocked at 94kph — knocked ball away from Mbemba, chased it, hit it with full explosive force of right boot, into near top corner; GK Mpasi didn't move a muscle
- Kane's equaliser: Declan Rice burst down right, crossed to Anthony Gordon (sub), who hung ball back in; Kane darted away from goal, leapt high, powered header into bottom corner
- Article compares Kane's 2nd goal to Bobby Thomson's 1951 "shot heard 'round the world"
- Kane's close relationship with Tom Brady — copied Brady's mindset, responsibility, single-mindedness
- Kane has two Bundesliga titles with Bayern Munich
- England were 1-0 down with 15 minutes left — would have been one of their most disappointing exits

### Tactical (Michael Cox analysis)
- DR Congo surprised England with 4-1-4-1 — nothing like 5-3-2 / 5-diamond-1 from group stage
- Similar to how Ghana played England (goalless draw matchday 2) — other opponents now copying it
- DR Congo manager Sebastien Desabre: "We wanted to shut down the centre better, to cut off the path to Anderson"
- DR Congo goal: RB Djed Spence pushed infield to track Noah Sadiki; Aaron Wan-Bissaka overlapped from other side; Brian Cipenga left completely unmarked at far post
- GK Lionel Mpasi was DR Congo's star performer — outstanding saves throughout
- Tuchel post-match: "keep pounding the rock, keep knocking, don't give in, do what we believe in"
- Tuchel's winger rotation plan: start Rashford/Madueke or Gordon/Saka, replace after 60-70 min with fresh legs
- Eberechi Eze came on for Spence; Bellingham pushed forward; Anderson sole holding midfielder
- Declan Rice shifted to right-back — played there on occasion for Arsenal last season; better crosser than England's recognised RB options
- Desabre on why they lost: "It took the best striker in the world"
- England accumulated over 1.0 xG by half-time despite being 1-0 down

### England injuries/absences
- RB Reece James (hamstring) and CB Jarell Quansah (ankle) out
- Deputy RB Tino Livramento lost before tournament to calf injury

*Source: The Athletic (Jack Pitt-Brooke — Kane piece; Michael Cox — tactical analysis)*
