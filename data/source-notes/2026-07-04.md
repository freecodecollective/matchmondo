# Source Notes -- July 4, 2026

## France 1-0 Paraguay (R16, Philadelphia)

### Result
- Mbappe penalty 70' (awarded after VAR review of Diego Gomez foul on Doue in the box)
- xG: Paraguay 0.13 - France 1.45
- Match stats: France 75.9% possession, 15 shots (5 on target) vs Paraguay 5 shots (0 on target), 12 corners to 2
- Paraguay completed just 54% of their passes -- lowest passing accuracy in a tournament knockout match in 60 years *(The Athletic, Austin Green)*
- France's first shot came after 22 minutes -- the longest wait for an attempt in a tournament knockout match since Opta records dating back to 1966 *(The Athletic, Adam Crafton)*
- Neither side hit the target in the first half
- Played under Extreme Heat Warning; kickoff temperature ~100F / 38.3C per the National Weather Service *(BBC Sport, Neil Johnston)*
- Record for a tournament game is 43C (Republic of Ireland vs Mexico, Florida, 1994) *(BBC Sport, Neil Johnston)*

### Mbappe Stats (post-match)
- 7 tournament goals this tournament, level with Messi in the Golden Boot race *(The Athletic, multiple; BBC Sport, Neil Johnston)*
- 19 goals in 19 tournament games all-time -- one behind Messi's all-time record (20) *(BBC Sport, Neil Johnston)*
- 11 knockout goals since 2018 -- more than Brazil (10), England (10), Portugal (9), and Spain (4) combined at knockouts *(BBC Sport, Neil Johnston)*
- Has scored 2+ goals in 7 different tournament matches, 3 more than any other player
- Now part of 9 tournament knockout wins

### Doue as Game-Changer
- Came on 61st minute and immediately won the penalty with a dribble through the box that Diego Gomez could only stop with a foul (knee-on-knee contact) *(The Athletic, Sebastian Stafford-Bloor; Austin Green)*
- "He had three Paraguay players closing in as he bobbed and weaved into the penalty area before he was tripped for the decisive penalty" *(The Athletic, Amy Lawrence)*
- Penalty call absolutely correct per VAR review; no question Doue was fouled *(The Athletic, Sebastian Stafford-Bloor)*

## Canada 0-3 Morocco (R16, Houston)

### Result
- Morocco beat Canada 3-0 with three second-half goals
- Morocco won despite having just 5 shots on goal -- fewest by a team winning a tournament knockout match on record *(BBC Sport, Emma Smith)*
- First half was the first in tournament history with more yellow cards than shots *(BBC Sport, Emma Smith)*
- Referee Michael Oliver produced 6 yellow cards before half-time; game then calmed, only 2 in second half *(BBC Sport, Dale Johnson analysis)*
- Morocco did not have a touch in the opposition box for the first 15 minutes for the second successive fixture *(BBC Sport, Emma Smith)*

### Morocco Stats
- Unbeaten in last 34 matches across all competitions (record carries asterisk re: 2026 AFCON final vs Senegal, awarded retroactively and being challenged in court) *(BBC Sport, Emma Smith)*
- Last loss: 1-0 to Kenya in August 2025 in African Nations Championship (tournament solely for players in Africa's domestic leagues) *(BBC Sport, Emma Smith)*
- Morocco have now won 4 tournament knockout matches (2 in 2022, 2 in 2026) -- as many as all other African nations combined (Cameroon, Senegal, Ghana, Egypt) *(BBC Sport, Emma Smith)*
- One more win matches their 2022 showing (first African nation to reach semi-finals)

### Key Players
- Brahim Diaz: 2 assists in this match, now 4 tournament assists total -- most by any African player *(BBC Sport, Emma Smith)*
- Achraf Hakimi: "arguably the world's best right back, was a constant menace both on the ball and in the Canadian players' faces" *(BBC Sport, Emma Smith)*
- Yassine Bounou (Bono): key save from Tani Oluwaseyi's early chance *(BBC Sport, Emma Smith; The Athletic, Joshua Kloke)*

### Canada's Tournament
- Canada's best chance came in 10th minute: Oluwaseyi turned on an Ali Ahmed pass outside the box, clear look at goal, saved by Bounou *(The Athletic, Joshua Kloke)*
- Stephen Eustaquio: "He said to us that probably that was the best first half he ever coached with us. To be able to get to the highest stage we were and to perform like that in the first half, I think it was a very good sign that the gap isn't that big." *(The Athletic, Joshua Kloke)*
- Alistair Johnston: "They saw a Canadian team that was not afraid of anyone, that played how I think we always want all of our Canadian teams to play." *(The Athletic, Joshua Kloke)*
- Jesse Marsch on Morocco: "They were bending a little bit but they didn't break." *(BBC Sport, Emma Smith)*
- In 2022, Canada finished 31st of 32 teams; this tournament was a transformation *(The Athletic, Joshua Kloke)*
- Young players who showed well for future: Luc de Fougerolles, Niko Sigur, Ali Ahmed, Moise Bombito *(The Athletic, Joshua Kloke)*
- Alphonso Davies returned to bench after managing hamstring problem; was expected to start replacing Richie Laryea *(Men in Blazers, Betty Clever)*
- Davies after R32 win vs South Africa: "It's honestly truly amazing. It brought tears to my eyes because this is something that's going to elevate the sport in the country so much." *(Men in Blazers, Betty Clever)*

### Morocco Investment Context
- Success underpinned by long-term investment from King Mohammed VI; academy and $65M training complex opened 2009 and 2019 *(BBC Sport, Emma Smith)*
- Mohamed Ouahbi: "It's not a surprise, we are no longer a surprise as of today. When people talk about Morocco they talk about a real contender, a major footballing nation." *(BBC Sport, Emma Smith)*
- Chris Sutton (BBC 5 Live): "Morocco were just not at their very best, and there are tougher tests to come. If France get through and Morocco perform like they did in the first half against a team like France, they will be crushed." *(BBC Sport, Emma Smith)*

## Offside Controversy

### Technology Overview
- Semi-Automated Offside Technology (SAOT): captures 100 frames per second using 10,000 data points on all 22 players *(The Athletic, Philip Buckingham)*
- Connected Ball Technology (CBT): Adidas Trionda ball contains 500Hz inertial measurement unit (IMU) motion sensor; delivers data 500 times per second to determine if a ball was touched by a player *(The Athletic, Philip Buckingham)*
- Premier League uses 5cm attacker grace when checking through SAOT *(The Athletic, Philip Buckingham)*

### Key Incidents
- Cristiano Ronaldo: goal vs Croatia in R32 disallowed -- shoulder narrowly ahead of last defender *(The Athletic, Philip Buckingham)*
- Colombia's Davinson Sanchez: late "winner" vs Portugal disallowed -- offside by the length of his toe *(The Athletic, Philip Buckingham)*
- Iran's Shoja Khalilzadeh: would-have-been qualifying goal vs Egypt disallowed by narrow VAR call *(The Athletic, Philip Buckingham)*
- Croatia goal vs Portugal: ruled out after CBT (ball chip) detected flick off Igor Matanovic's hair en route to Mario Pasalic, who set up Josko Gvardiol. Shown by a "spike on the sensor" *(The Athletic, Philip Buckingham)*
- Zlatko Dalic (Croatia coach): "You were able to see to what extent emotions have been literally killed, and altogether these decisions take you back and actually take the joy out of football." *(The Athletic, Philip Buckingham)*

### Wenger's "Daylight Offside" Proposal
- Arsene Wenger (head of global development): wants player ruled onside if any part of body is in line with last outfield defender, rather than having to be behind the line *(The Athletic, Philip Buckingham)*
- Canadian Premier League (CPL) given green light in March to trial daylight offsides for current season; first goal scored under "Wenger's law" in April *(The Athletic, Philip Buckingham)*
- CPL re-refereed all tournament games and concluded 17 additional goals would have been scored under daylight interpretation *(The Athletic, Philip Buckingham)*
- Any change must go through IFAB (International Football Association Board); requires three-quarter majority -- FAs of England, Wales, Scotland, Northern Ireland get 1 vote each, governing body gets 4 *(The Athletic, Philip Buckingham)*
