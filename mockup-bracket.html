🏆 Knockout bracket — mobile options

A full bracket is wide; phones are narrow. Here are four ways the app could show the knockout rounds. Each demo is live. (Projected sample results.)

The challenge: the real bracket runs Round of 32 → 16 → Quarters → Semis → Final across the screen. On a phone you can’t show it all at once and keep it readable. These options each make a different trade. — for Luis & Chris
ARound by round (tabs)
A tab per round; each shows that round’s matchups as a clean vertical list. The most phone-native — no horizontal scrolling, everything readable. (Swipe between rounds would feel the same.)
Good for: readability, taps to teams. Trade-off: you don’t see the whole bracket shape at once.
BClassic bracket (scrolls sideways)
The familiar full bracket — like ESPN / Fox on the web — that you scroll left↔right. Keeps the connected shape, but text is small and you do a lot of scrolling.
← swipe sideways to see all rounds →
Good for: the “real bracket” feel, seeing connections. Trade-off: small text, fiddly on a phone.
CSplit the draw (top / bottom halves)
Break the bracket into its two halves stacked vertically, each a small left-to-right bracket that fits the width, meeting at the Final. Shows structure without one giant scroll.
Good for: structure + fits width. Trade-off: the two halves are split apart, still a bit dense.
DFollow a team (path to the final)
Pick a team and see just their road through the knockouts as a vertical timeline. Personal and engaging — pairs nicely with one of the above as the “full” view.
Good for: engagement, “my team’s route.” Trade-off: shows one team, not the field.
