📅 Schedule — top-tab options

The Schedule tab gets 3 tabs at the top: 3-Day, Full Schedule, Brackets. The old stage-filter row becomes a Jump to today button. Here are four ways to style the tabs — tap to try.

Picking: the labels are fixed (3-Day / Full Schedule / Brackets) — “Full Schedule” is the long one, so each style is shown handling it. Below the tabs is the new Jump to today button (replacing the stage pills) and a peek of the list. — for Chris
ASegmented capsule
Matches the app’s existing Standings (Groups / Players) control — one filled capsule, three equal segments. Most consistent with what’s already in the app.
⚽

Schedule

Good for: consistency, clearly “pick one of three.” Trade-off: “Full Schedule” is tight in a third.
BUnderline tabs
Text tabs with an underline under the active one — like a lot of iOS/news apps. Lighter weight, and long labels breathe more.
⚽

Schedule

Good for: clean, handles long labels. Trade-off: a bit less “tappable button” feel than a capsule.
CFilled pills
Rounded pills (like the stage chips you’re removing, repurposed as the main tabs). Sized to content, left-aligned. Could scroll if more tabs are added later.
⚽

Schedule

Good for: friendly, flexible, fits any label. Trade-off: not equal-width; reads a little less like a top-level switch.
DSegmented + icons
Same capsule as A, but each tab gets a small icon above the label. More scannable; the icon helps when the label is tight.
⚽

Schedule

Good for: scannability, eases the tight label. Trade-off: emoji icons are placeholders; SF Symbols in-app would look cleaner.
