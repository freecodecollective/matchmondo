⚽ Formation — half-pitch (starting XI)

ESPN-screenshot aesthetic, but rendered as the team's own half of the field — like an aerial frame the instant before kickoff. Half-circle at the centerline, goal at the far end. Four variants.

Why half-pitch The full-pitch view squeezes 22 players into one rectangle, which crushes labels and makes each player tiny. Showing only the team you're looking at — on their own half — gives every chip and label twice the room. The half-circle at the centerline tells you instantly which way they kick.

Defensive aerial — you see the team set up to attack downward
Closest to the screenshot's layout. Half-circle at the bottom; the team kicks down off the screen.

How to read these: the half-circle at the centerline tells you which way the team attacks. Variants 1 and 2 are the closest to the ESPN screenshot's vertical layout — V1 puts the team's goal at the top (familiar from defensive replays), V2 flips it (attacking up the field, which reads more intuitively). V3 is for wider devices. V4 is the bonus — no half is hidden, but each team is in its starting half, like watching from the stands two seconds before kickoff.
