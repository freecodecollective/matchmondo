First, the “group winner” crown 👑

Chris asked whether Mexico & USA should have a crown.

It’s working — just not earned yet

The crown means a team has mathematically won its group (guaranteed 1st). Mexico and USA have clinched a top‑2 spot (shown green = “through”), but with one game left they could still be passed on goal difference and finish 2nd — so no crown yet. It’ll appear automatically once 1st place is locked (realistically after the final group games). This matches our “only color it once it’s settled” approach.

Decision A: keep the crown strict (= won the group), or loosen it to mean “currently top of the group”? Recommendation: keep it strict.

8‑Second Goalkeeper Rule

If a keeper controls the ball with their hands for more than 8 seconds, the opponent gets a corner kick — the referee counts down the last 5 seconds on their fingers. (This is the headline new law and we don’t show it at all — our “5‑second” card is a garbled version of it.)

Captain‑only with the referee

Only the team captain may approach the referee to discuss a decision; teammates who crowd the official can be cautioned.

Referee announcements

Referees explain the final video‑review decision over the stadium PA, and wear body cameras for broadcast. Video review is also expanded to wrongly awarded corners, an incorrect second yellow, and mistaken identity.
